One day, in the late ‘90s, I came home from school to find a Gateway Computer in
the living room; the Windows 98 screensaver bounced around, waiting for me.  It
wasn’t something I asked for, in fact, I’m not even sure I understood what
computers were.  But there it was, and I was obsessed.

First I learned to type, then I made a webpage for the family.  Everybody had
their own page and bio that told the world who they were.  If only I knew what a
web host was.

Next, I discovered GeoCities and made a webpage for my high school.  It was no
<a rel="nofollow" href="https://gossipgirl.fandom.com/wiki/Blog:Recent_posts" target="_blank" title="Gossip Girl Fandom Blog">
  "Gossip Girl."</a>
I’m not even sure if anybody saw it, but I was a freshman and it was fun.
Then, in college, one of the assignments was to create a Wordpress blog and
portfolio site before graduation.  Of course, after I got my grade for the
class, it was never maintained, and I never thought to download a copy the
database.  I did buy my first domain, though.

And from that point forward, my site was the go-to place for absolutely
nothing.  At most, you could find three stale projects I once worked on.
Before starting a new job, I’d redesign the site.  Rinse and repeat.

But I always had a vision that one day my are would have visitors and a reason
for people to visit.  **One day is today!**

I’m proud to announce that my site is finally set up in a way that allows me to
share content in a way that means something to me.  It’s not a social media
feed, it’s not a random think piece on someone else’s server.  It’s here, and
it’s mine!  And today, I’m finally ready to share it with the world!

Hello, World!
