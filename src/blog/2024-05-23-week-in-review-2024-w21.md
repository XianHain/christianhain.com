Hey, what's up!

So… now that this blog is set up and ready for content, it's time to actually
write about things. I'm hesitant to put myself on any strict schedule because I
don't want to produce sloppy content for the sake of ritual, but it would be
good to get into the habit of writing and posting; I think weekly roundups are a
good place to start.

In the meantime, I'm working on a couple of long-form essays. Until those are
ready, I thought starting with a light recap of things that caught my eye or
earned my attention this week.

Okay, enough ado!

## JavaScript Registry (JSR)
<a rel="nofollow" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MFCn4ce5dVc" title="YouTube: Ryan Dahl introduces JSR at DevWorld 2024" target="_blank">
  Announced on May 21, 2023
</a>,
JSR aims to modernize how JavaScript packages are published. It's currently
billed as a "superset" of
<a rel="nofollow" href="https://www.npmjs.com/" title="NPM's Website" target="_blank">
  NPM.
</a> However, it exists because it is difficult for maintainers to publish their
packages to NPM, specifically when it comes to choosing to distribute in
CommonJS, ESModules, or both.

This has the potential to take off and replace NPM for a few reasons:
1. It's created by 
   <a rel="nofollow" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryan_Dahl" title="Ryan Dahl Wikipedia" target="_blank">
     Ryan Dahl</a>,
   creator of 
   <a rel="nofollow" href="https://nodejs.org/en" title="Node's Website" target="_blank">
     Node.js</a>
   and
   <a rel="nofollow" href="https://deno.com/" title="Deno's Website" target="_blank">
     Deno.js</a>
2. Deno.js is gaining traction because it leverages modern JavaScript and
   TypeScript techniques
3. <a rel="nofollow" href="https://jsr.io/@doctor/encoding-stream" title="JSR: @doctor/encoding-stream" target="_blank">
     jsr.io's Detail Pages
   </a> 
   promote quality development practices, offers copy/paste functionality for
   multiple build systems like Yarn
   (<a rel="nofollow" href="https://github.com/jsr-io/jsr/issues/546" title="GitHub: Persist 'Use with' selection" target="_blank">
     it even remembers your preferred system
   </a>),
   and compatible packages can also be imported directly in the browser 
   (combining the power of systems like NPM and 
   <a rel="nofollow" href="https://www.jsdelivr.com/" title="JSDelivr's Website" target="_blank">
     JSDelivr
   </a>).

If you don't want to watch the full announcement video, here's a 
<a rel= "nofollow" href= "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gOfOSRertMA" title= "YouTube: Another JS Registry. Seriously?!" target="_blank">
  6-minute overview YouTube video</a>.

## Arc Browser
I was initially skeptical of a new browser, but after a few weeks of playing
with Arc, it's earned a spot on my roster. I love
<a rel="nofollow" href="https://github.com/jsr-io/jsr/issues/546" title="A GitHub Example" target="_blank">
  how it looks in screenshots</a>.

This is what my current roster is looking like
1. Safari (my main) for general browsing
2. Firefox for development
3. Arc for screenshots and recordings
4. <a rel="nofollow" href="https://polypane.app" title="Polypane's Website" target="_blank">
     Polypane
   </a>
   for heavy CSS and Responsive lifting
5. Chrome for G-Suite (Google Meet mostly)

I haven't played with Arc's AI features, but here's 
<a href="https://arc.net/gift/d0d83acb" title="A friend is sharing Arc with you!" target="_blank">
  my share link if you want to give it a go</a>.

## Made by Human, Not by AI
One of the main reasons I'm starting to take blogging seriously is how many
people are using AI to spam posts on LinkedIn, Hashnode, etc. I don't want to
read the results of someone else's GPT prompt, and I'm willing to bet I'm not
alone.
That's why I'm supporting <a href="https://notbyai.fyi/hi/christianhain/" title="My 'Not By AI' Page" target="_blank">
  Not by AI</a>.

Of course, I'm not here to yuck anyone's yum. If you want to use GPT to
summarize GPT-posts and then send a GPT-email to your buddies and colleagues so
they can reply with GPT-response, have fun!

But anyway, I'm supporting a human-first community because I value original
thoughts, so if you want a human take on the human world, bookmark this site or
something. I'll probably add an RSS feed over the weekend.
